    • otters are carnivorous mammals
    River otters and giant otters hunt by using their vibrissae to detect movements of prey in the water. They catch prey with their teeth. otters are know to eat slow-moving fishes and marine invertebrates including crabs, sea urchins, abalones, clams, mussels, and snails.

    unlike other mammals they are smaller and have thick furry coats
    Sea otters thick fur coat keeps them warm in cold water. The thick fur on their coats aids in the prevention of wet and cold, as well as insulation.

    There are 13 extant otter species all are semiaquatic, aquatic, or marine, with diets based on fish and invertebrates
